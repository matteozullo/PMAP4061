# Schedule

The modules for this course define the content which students are expected to master at the end of this course. The textbook might be used to reinforce learning but its use is not required. Coversely, readings are mandatory unless marked as "Optional".
The outline for the course is displayed below indicating class meetings, materials, and assignment release and due dates.

### M2: The Rational Model

#### M1.2: Assumptions
* *Keys*: methodological individualism, utility maximization, micro and macro level
* [Arrow, K. J. (1994). Methodological Individualism and Social Knowledge. *The American Economic Review*, 84(2):1–9.](https://www.jstor.org/stable/2117792)
* [Hechter, M. & Kanazawa, S. (1997). Sociological Rational Choice Theory. *Annual Review of Sociology*, 23(1):191–214.](https://doi.org/10.1146/annurev.soc.23.1.191)

#### M2.2: Extensions
* *Keys*: bounded rationality, game theory, general equilibrium
* *Readings*:
	* [Lindblom, C. E. (1959). The Science of "Muddling" Through. *Public Administration Review*, 19(2):77–88.](https://www.jstor.org/stable/973677)
	* [Hermans, L., Cunningham, S., & Slinger, J. (2014). The Usefulness of Game Theory as a Method for Policy Evaluation. *Evaluation*, 20(1):10–25.](https://doi.org/10.1177/1356389013516052)
	* [Zullo, M. (2020). Game Theory at Work: Italy’s "Gentle" Lockdowns. *Towards Data Science*](https://towardsdatascience.com/game-theory-at-work-italys-gentle-lockdowns-caab8c4c4722) [Optional]

#### M2.3: Market Failures
* *Keys*: externalities, informational asymmetries, transaction costs, public goods
* *Readings*:
	* [Moe, T. M. (1984). The New Economics of Organization. *American Journal of  Political Science*, 28(4):739–777.](https://doi.org/10.2307/2110997)
	* [Coase, R. H. (1960). The Problem of Social Cost. *Journal of Law and Economics*, 3:1–44.](https://doi.org/10.1086/466560)

* *Keys*: punctuated equilibrium theory, advocacy coalition framework, agenda setting, policy diffusion
* *Readings*:

	* [True, J. L., Jones, B. D., & Baumgartner, F. R. (2014). Punctuated-Equilibrium Theory: Explaining Stability and Change in Public Policymaking. In Weible, C. M. and Sabatier, P. A., editors, *Theories of the Policy Process*, chapter 6, pages 155–187. Routledge, New York, NY.](https://doi.org/10.4324/9780429494284)

#### M4.2: Behavioral Social Sciences
* *Keys*: Asch experiments, biases, nudges, neuroeconomics
* *Readings*:
	* [Behavioural Insights Team (2017). *A Review of Optimism Bias, Planning Fallacy, Sunk Cost Bias and Groupthink in Project Delivery and Organisational Decision Making*. Commissioned Report. Department of Transport.](https://www.bi.team/wp-content/uploads/2018/02/Lit-Review-exploration-of-behavioural-biases-in-DfT-PD_July_2017.pdf)
	* [Fox, J. (2015). The Elusive Benefits of Common Parts. *Harvard Business Review*, pages 78–85.](https://hbr.org/2015/05/from-economic-man-to-behavioral-economics)


### M5: Cost-Benefit Analysis

#### M5.1: Net Present Value
* *Keys*: net present value, time discounting, monetizing
* *Readings*:
	* [Miller, G. J. & Robbins, D. (2007). Cost-Benefit Analysis. In Fischer, F. and Miller, G. J., editors, *Handbook of Public Policy Analysis*, chapter 31, pages 465– 480. Routledge, New York, NY.](https://doi.org/10.4324/9781315093192)
	* [Wang, S. J., Middleton, B., Prosser, L. A., Bardon, C. G., Spurr, C. D., Carchidi,  P. J., Kittler, A. F., Goldszer, R. C., Fairchild, D. G., Sussman, A. J., Kuperman, G. J., & Bates, D. W. (2003). A Cost-Benefit Analysis of Electronic Medical Records in Primary Care. *The American Journal of Medicine*, 114(5):397–403.](https://doi.org/10.1016/S0002-9343(03)00057-3)

#### M5.2: Issues
* *Keys*: value of statistical life, hedonic pricing, short-termism
* *Readings*:
	* [Frank, R. H. (2000). Why is Cost-Benefit Analysis So Controversial? *The Journal of Legal Studies*, 29(S2):913–930.](https://doi.org/10.1111/j.1468-0297.2006.01072.x)
	* [Ashenfelter, O. (2006). Measuring the Value of a Statistical Life: Problems and Prospects. The Economic Journal, 116(510):C10–C23.](https://doi.org/10.1086/468099)


### M6: Methods for Policy Analysis

#### M6.1: Research Designs
* *Keys*: causal models, internal and external validity, threats to validity
* *Readings*:
	* [Stinchcombe, A. L. (1987). *Constructing Social Theories*, chapter 2, pages 2173–2181. University of Chicago Press, Chicago, IL.](https://press.uchicago.edu/ucp/books/book/chicago/C/bo3618306.html)
	* [Slack, M. K. & Draugalis Jr, J. R. (2001). Establishing the Internal and External Validity of Experimental Studies. American Journal of Health-System Pharmacy, 58(22):2173–2181.](https://doi.org/10.1093/ajhp/58.22.2173)
	* [Stanley, J. & Campbell, D. (1963). *Experimental and Quasi-Experimental Designs for Research*. Cengage Learning.](https://www.sfu.ca/~palys/Campbell&Stanley-1959-Exptl&QuasiExptlDesignsForResearch.pdf) [Optional]

#### M6.2: Quantitative Methods
* *Keys*: identification, quasi-experiments, synthetic control
* *Readings*
	* [Athey, S. & Imbens, G. W. (2017). The State of Applied Econometrics: Causality and Policy evaluation. *Journal of Economic Perspectives*, 31(2):3–32.](https://doi.org/10.1257/jep.31.2.3)
	* [Abadie, A. & Cattaneo, M. D. (2018). Econometric Methods for Program Evaluation. *Annual Review of Economics*, 10:465–503.](https://doi.org/10.1146/annurev-economics-080217-053402)

#### M6.3: Data Science
* *Keys*: big data, algorithms, machine learning
* *Readings*:
	* [Athey, S. (2017). Beyond Prediction: Using Big Data for Policy Problems. *Science*, 355(6324):483–485.](https://doi.org/10.1126/science.aal4321)
	* [Blumenstock, J., Cadamuro, G., & On, R. (2015). Predicting Poverty and Wealth from Mobile Phone Metadata. *Science*, 350(6264):1073–1076.](https://doi.org/10.1126/science.aac4420)
	* [Arnold, K. E. & Pistilli, M. D. (2012). Course Signals at Purdue: Using Learning Analytics to Increase Student Success. In *Proceedings of the 2nd international Conference on Learning Analytics and Knowledge*, pages 267–270.](https://doi.org/10.1145/2330601.2330666) [Optional]
